Use this calculator to estimate the minimum profitable price for a product or service. Useful for ensuring your pricing covers all costs and delivers your target profit margin.

## What this calculator does

The minimum profitable price calculator helps you determine the lowest price you can charge while still making a profit.

It uses:

- total cost per unit
- desired profit margin

This gives you the **minimum selling price** required to stay profitable.

## How to use the minimum profitable price calculator

1. Enter your **total cost per unit** (including all costs)  
2. Enter your **desired profit margin (%)**  
3. The calculator will return your minimum profitable price  

Ensure your cost includes all relevant expenses for accuracy.

## Minimum profitable price formula

**Minimum Price = Cost ÷ (1 − Profit Margin)**

Where:

- **Cost** = total cost per unit (product, shipping, fees, etc.)  
- **Profit Margin** = desired margin expressed as a decimal  
- **Minimum Price** = lowest price you can charge profitably  

## Example calculation

If:

- Cost = **500**  
- Desired profit margin = **40% (0.40)**  

Then:

- Minimum price = **500 ÷ (1 − 0.40)**  
- Minimum price = **500 ÷ 0.60**  
- Minimum price = **833.33**  

This means you must charge at least 833.33 to achieve a 40% margin.

## What is minimum profitable price?

Minimum profitable price is the lowest price you can charge while still covering costs and achieving your target profit margin.

It ensures that every sale contributes positively to your business.

## What costs should be included?

To calculate accurately, include all relevant costs:

- product or manufacturing cost  
- shipping and fulfilment  
- platform or payment fees  
- marketing and acquisition costs  
- packaging and handling  
- overhead allocation  

Incomplete cost inputs lead to underpricing.

## Why this calculation matters

Understanding your minimum profitable price helps you:

- avoid selling at a loss  
- set sustainable pricing  
- protect profit margins  
- plan promotions and discounts safely  
- make better product and supplier decisions  

Without this, businesses often underprice and lose money.

## Minimum price vs markup vs margin

These concepts are related but different:

- **Minimum price** ensures profitability  
- **Markup** is based on cost  
- **Margin** is based on selling price

## Common mistakes when calculating minimum price

Common mistakes include:

- excluding key costs (ads, shipping, fees)  
- confusing markup with margin  
- setting margin too low for sustainability  
- ignoring returns and refunds  
- using inconsistent cost data  

Always base calculations on full, real costs.
