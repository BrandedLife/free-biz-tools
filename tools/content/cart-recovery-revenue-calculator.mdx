## What this calculator does

The cart recovery revenue calculator helps estimate how much revenue can be recovered from abandoned carts.

It is useful for:

- email automation planning
- SMS recovery analysis
- ecommerce forecasting
- retention revenue review

## Formula

**Recovered Revenue = Abandoned Carts × Recovery Rate × Average Order Value**

Where:

- **Abandoned Carts** = number of incomplete purchases
- **Recovery Rate** = percentage of carts recovered
- **Average Order Value** = average value of each recovered order
- **Recovered Revenue** = estimated revenue won back from abandoned carts

## Example calculation

If:

- Abandoned carts = **300**
- Recovery rate = **12%**
- Average order value = **950**

Then:

- Recovered orders = **300 × 0.12**
- Recovered orders = **36**
- Recovered revenue = **36 × 950**
- Recovered revenue = **34200.00**

## What is cart recovery revenue?

Cart recovery revenue is the estimated sales value recovered from shoppers who abandoned checkout but later completed their purchase.

## Why cart recovery revenue matters

This calculation helps businesses:

- estimate recovery flow value
- justify retention tools
- improve email and SMS strategy
- identify revenue already within reach

## When to use this calculator

Use this calculator when you want to:

- forecast abandoned cart flow returns
- compare recovery campaign performance
- plan retention initiatives
- estimate lost revenue recovery potential

## Common mistakes

Common mistakes include:

- overstating the recovery rate
- using the wrong average order value
- treating recovered orders as fully incremental when they may overlap
- ignoring discounts used in recovery campaigns

## Cart recovery revenue vs cart abandonment rate

These are closely related.

- **Cart recovery revenue** estimates sales won back
- **Cart abandonment rate** measures how many shoppers leave without buying
