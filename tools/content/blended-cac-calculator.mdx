Use this calculator to estimate blended customer acquisition cost.

## What this calculator does

The blended CAC calculator helps measure the true average cost to acquire a customer across all marketing channels.

It is useful for:

- marketing efficiency analysis
- paid and organic channel review
- customer acquisition planning
- growth benchmarking

## Formula

**Blended CAC = Total Marketing Spend ÷ New Customers Acquired**

Where:

- **Total Marketing Spend** = total spend across all relevant acquisition activity
- **New Customers Acquired** = total new customers gained in the period
- **Blended CAC** = average acquisition cost per customer

## Example calculation

If:

- Total marketing spend = **120000**
- New customers acquired = **240**

Then:

- Blended CAC = **120000 ÷ 240**
- Blended CAC = **500.00**

## What is blended CAC?

Blended CAC measures the average cost to acquire each new customer when all acquisition costs are considered together.

## Why blended CAC matters

This calculation helps businesses:

- understand real acquisition efficiency
- compare periods more accurately
- set realistic growth targets
- assess overall marketing performance

## When to use this calculator

Use this calculator when you want to:

- review acquisition costs
- compare channel performance at a high level
- forecast customer growth costs
- monitor marketing efficiency over time

## Common mistakes

Common mistakes include:

- excluding agency or software costs
- mixing different time periods
- counting leads instead of customers
- ignoring organic and non-paid acquisition spend

## Blended CAC vs CAC

These are closely related.

- **Blended CAC** includes total marketing cost across all channels
- **CAC** may sometimes refer to a narrower paid acquisition figure
