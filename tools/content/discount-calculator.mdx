## What this calculator does

The discount calculator helps you determine how much a product costs after a discount is applied.

It calculates:

- discount amount
- final price

## Discount Formula

**Discount Amount = Original Price × Discount Percentage**

**Final Price = Original Price − Discount Amount**

## Example calculation

If:

- Original price = **200**
- Discount = **20%**

Then:

Discount amount = **40**

Final price = **160**

## What is a discount?

A discount is a reduction in the original price of a product or service.

Discounts are often used to:

- attract customers
- clear inventory
- promote sales events
- increase purchase volume

## Why discounts matter

Discounts influence buying decisions and can increase sales, but they also affect profit margins.

Businesses should balance:

- competitive pricing
- profitability
- customer expectations

## When to use this calculator

Use this calculator when you want to:

- calculate sale prices
- evaluate promotion strategies
- compare different discount levels
- estimate revenue impact

## Common mistakes

Common mistakes include:

- applying multiple discounts incorrectly
- confusing discount percentage and final price
- forgetting how discounts affect profit margin

## Discount vs markup

Discount reduces price from the original selling price.

Markup increases price from cost.

Both are important when managing pricing strategies.
