## What this calculator does

The hourly rate calculator helps freelancers, consultants, and service businesses set a sustainable hourly rate.

It is useful for:

- pricing services
- setting consulting rates
- freelance planning
- profitability checks

## Hourly Rate Formula

**Hourly Rate = (Target Income + Overhead) ÷ Billable Hours**

Where:

- **Target Income** = desired annual income
- **Overhead** = annual business costs
- **Billable Hours** = hours you expect to charge clients for

## Example calculation

If:

- Target income = **80000**
- Overhead = **20000**
- Billable hours = **1600**

Then:

- Hourly rate = **(80000 + 20000) ÷ 1600**
- Hourly rate = **62.50**

## What is an hourly rate?

An hourly rate is the amount charged for each billable hour of work.

It should cover income goals, business costs, and enough margin for sustainability.

## Why hourly rate matters

Hourly rate helps service businesses:

- price work more accurately
- protect profitability
- avoid undercharging
- plan income with more confidence

## Common mistakes

Common mistakes include:

- overestimating billable hours
- ignoring overhead
- basing pricing only on desired salary
- not reviewing rates regularly

## Hourly rate vs contractor rate

These are similar but not identical.

- **Hourly rate** often focuses on income plus overhead
- **Contractor rate** may also include desired profit margin
