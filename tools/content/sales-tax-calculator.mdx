## What this calculator does

The sales tax calculator shows the tax added to a price and the final total.

It is useful for:

- checkout calculations
- pricing reviews
- invoicing
- ecommerce tax checks

## Sales Tax Formula

**Sales Tax = Amount × Tax Rate**

**Total Including Tax = Amount + Sales Tax**

Where:

- **Amount** = price before tax
- **Tax Rate** = sales tax percentage
- **Sales Tax** = added tax amount

## Example calculation

If:

- Amount = **100**
- Sales tax rate = **8%**

Then:

- Sales tax = **8**
- Total including tax = **108**

## What is sales tax?

Sales tax is a consumption tax added to the sale of goods and services in many regions.

It increases the final amount paid by the customer.

## Why sales tax matters

Sales tax matters because it helps businesses:

- price products correctly
- avoid undercharging or overcharging
- prepare invoices properly
- understand final customer totals

## Common mistakes

Common mistakes include:

- using the wrong tax rate
- forgetting local tax differences
- confusing VAT with sales tax
- applying tax after discounts incorrectly

## Sales tax vs VAT

These are similar but not identical.

- **Sales tax** is usually added at the final sale
- **VAT** is applied at multiple stages of the value chain
