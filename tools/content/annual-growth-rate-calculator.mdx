## What this calculator does

The annual growth rate calculator helps estimate the yearly growth rate between a starting value and an ending value over multiple years.

It is useful for:

- long-term planning
- investment analysis
- forecasting
- strategic review

## Formula

**Annual Growth Rate = (End Value ÷ Start Value)^(1 ÷ Years) - 1**

Where:

- **Start Value** = value at the beginning of the period
- **End Value** = value at the end of the period
- **Years** = number of years in the period
- **Annual Growth Rate** = average yearly growth rate

## Example calculation

If:

- Start value = **1000**
- End value = **2000**
- Years = **3**

Then:

- Annual growth rate = **(2000 ÷ 1000)^(1 ÷ 3) - 1**
- Annual growth rate = **25.99%**

## What is annual growth rate?

Annual growth rate shows the average rate at which a metric grew each year over a period.

## Common mistakes

Common mistakes include:

- using the wrong number of years
- comparing mismatched values
- confusing CAGR with simple average growth
- ignoring volatility between years

## Annual growth rate vs monthly growth rate

These are closely related.

- **Annual growth rate** smooths growth across years
- **Monthly growth rate** focuses on short-term change
