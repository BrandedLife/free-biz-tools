Use this calculator to estimate contribution margin based on revenue and variable costs.

## What this calculator does

The contribution margin calculator helps you see how much revenue remains after variable costs are covered.

It is useful for:

- break-even analysis
- pricing decisions
- product profitability checks
- cost control

## Contribution Margin Formula

**Contribution Margin = Revenue − Variable Costs**

**Contribution Margin % = (Contribution Margin ÷ Revenue) × 100**

Where:

- **Revenue** = total sales income
- **Variable Costs** = costs that change with sales volume
- **Contribution Margin** = amount available to cover fixed costs and profit

## Example calculation

If:

- Revenue = **5000**
- Variable costs = **3000**

Then:

- Contribution margin = **2000**
- Contribution margin % = **40%**

## What is contribution margin?

Contribution margin shows how much money remains after variable costs are deducted from revenue.

That remaining amount contributes toward fixed costs and profit.

## Why contribution margin matters

Contribution margin helps businesses:

- understand product viability
- measure efficiency
- support pricing decisions
- improve break-even planning

## Common mistakes

Common mistakes include:

- including fixed costs as variable costs
- using inaccurate revenue figures
- ignoring discounts or returns
- confusing contribution margin with profit margin

## Contribution margin vs profit margin

These are different measures.

- **Contribution margin** focuses on variable costs
- **Profit margin** reflects profit relative to revenue

Contribution margin is especially useful for break-even and operational planning.
