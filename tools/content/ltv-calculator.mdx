Use this calculator to estimate customer lifetime value based on purchase value, purchase frequency, and lifespan.

## What this calculator does

The LTV calculator estimates how much revenue a customer generates over the full relationship with a business.

It is useful for:

- customer value analysis
- retention planning
- acquisition strategy
- SaaS and ecommerce forecasting

## LTV Formula

**LTV = Average Purchase Value × Purchase Frequency × Customer Lifespan**

Where:

- **Average Purchase Value** = average amount spent per order
- **Purchase Frequency** = average number of purchases per period
- **Customer Lifespan** = how long a customer stays active

## Example calculation

If:

- Average purchase value = **50**
- Purchase frequency = **4**
- Customer lifespan = **3 years**

Then:

- LTV = **50 × 4 × 3**
- LTV = **600**

## What is customer lifetime value?

Customer lifetime value is the estimated total revenue a customer generates over the entire relationship with a business.

It helps show the long-term value of acquiring and retaining customers.

## Why LTV matters

LTV helps businesses:

- understand customer value
- compare acquisition costs to long-term revenue
- improve marketing efficiency
- make better retention decisions

## When to use this calculator

Use this calculator when you want to:

- estimate long-term customer value
- compare LTV to CAC
- improve retention strategy
- evaluate subscription or repeat-purchase models

## Common mistakes

Common mistakes include:

- using unrealistic customer lifespan assumptions
- ignoring changes in purchase behavior
- comparing LTV without context
- confusing revenue with profit

## LTV vs CAC

These metrics are often used together.

- **LTV** shows the long-term value of a customer
- **CAC** shows how much it costs to acquire that customer

A healthy business usually aims for LTV to be significantly higher than CAC.
