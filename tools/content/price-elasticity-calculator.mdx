Use this calculator to estimate price elasticity of demand based on changes in price and quantity sold.

## What this calculator does

The price elasticity calculator helps measure how sensitive demand is to price changes.

It is useful for:

- pricing strategy
- demand forecasting
- revenue modeling
- market analysis

## Price Elasticity Formula

**Elasticity = % Change in Quantity ÷ % Change in Price**

Where:

- **% Change in Quantity** = change in quantity sold relative to old quantity
- **% Change in Price** = change in price relative to old price
- **Elasticity** = sensitivity of demand to price

## Example calculation

If price increases by **10%** and quantity sold falls by **20%**:

- Elasticity = **−20% ÷ 10%**
- Elasticity = **−2.0**

## What is price elasticity?

Price elasticity measures how much customer demand changes when price changes.

It helps show whether customers are highly price-sensitive or relatively insensitive.

## Why price elasticity matters

Price elasticity helps businesses:

- set better prices
- forecast demand changes
- understand customer behavior
- improve revenue strategy

## When to use this calculator

Use this calculator when you want to:

## Common mistakes

Common mistakes include:

- using short-term data only
- comparing unrelated time periods
- ignoring outside factors like seasonality
- assuming elasticity is constant in all situations

## Elastic vs inelastic demand

Elasticity results are often interpreted like this:

- **Elastic demand** means quantity changes strongly when price changes
- **Inelastic demand** means quantity changes only slightly
