Use this calculator to estimate annual depreciation.

## What this calculator does

The depreciation calculator helps estimate annual straight-line depreciation for an asset.

It is useful for:

- accounting estimates
- budgeting
- asset planning
- financial analysis

## Formula

**Annual Depreciation = (Asset Cost - Salvage Value) ÷ Useful Life**

Where:

- **Asset Cost** = original cost of the asset
- **Salvage Value** = estimated value at the end of useful life
- **Useful Life** = expected number of years used
- **Annual Depreciation** = yearly depreciation expense

## Example calculation

If:

- Asset cost = **100000**
- Salvage value = **10000**
- Useful life = **5**

Then:

- Annual depreciation = **(100000 - 10000) ÷ 5**
- Annual depreciation = **18000**

## What is depreciation?

Depreciation is the accounting process of spreading an asset’s cost over its useful life.

## Why depreciation matters

This calculation helps businesses:

- estimate non-cash expense

- support budgeting

## Common mistakes

Common mistakes include:

- ignoring salvage value
- using the wrong useful life
- mixing tax and accounting methods
- confusing depreciation with cash cost

## Depreciation vs amortization

These are closely related.

- **Depreciation** applies to tangible assets
- **Amortization** applies to intangible assets
