## What this calculator does

The profit margin calculator helps you measure how much profit is left from revenue after costs are deducted.

It is useful for:

- pricing products
- reviewing profitability
- comparing product lines
- planning discounts and promotions

## Profit Margin Formula

**Profit Margin (%) = ((Revenue − Cost) ÷ Revenue) × 100**

Where:

- **Revenue** = total sales income
- **Cost** = total direct cost
- **Profit** = revenue minus cost

## Example calculation

If:

- Revenue = **1000**
- Cost = **700**

Then:

- Profit = **300**
- Profit Margin = **(300 ÷ 1000) × 100**
- Profit Margin = **30%**

## What is profit margin?

Profit margin is the percentage of revenue that remains after costs are covered.

It shows how efficiently a business turns sales into profit.

## Why profit margin matters

Profit margin matters because it helps you:

- see whether a product is worth selling
- compare different products or services
- understand pricing strength
- plan for growth and marketing spend

## Common mistakes

Common mistakes include:

- confusing profit margin with markup
- excluding important costs
- comparing margin across very different business types
- using revenue figures after discounts without updating costs

## Profit margin vs markup

These two metrics are often confused.

- **Profit margin** is based on revenue
- **Markup** is based on cost

A 50% markup is not the same as a 50% profit margin.
