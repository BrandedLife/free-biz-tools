Use this calculator to estimate revenue growth between two periods.

## What this calculator does

The revenue growth calculator measures how much revenue has increased or decreased between two periods.

It is useful for:

- performance reviews
- business reporting
- forecasting
- growth analysis

## Revenue Growth Formula

**Revenue Growth % = ((New Revenue − Old Revenue) ÷ Old Revenue) × 100**

Where:

- **Old Revenue** = revenue from the earlier period
- **New Revenue** = revenue from the later period
- **Revenue Growth %** = percentage increase or decrease

## Example calculation

If revenue grows from **10000** to **12000**, growth is:

- **((12000 − 10000) ÷ 10000) × 100**
- **20%**

## What is revenue growth?

Revenue growth measures how much a business’s revenue changes over time.

It is one of the most common ways to assess business momentum.

## Why revenue growth matters

Revenue growth helps businesses:

- track performance trends
- evaluate strategy changes
- compare periods
- understand business momentum

## When to use this calculator

Use this calculator when you want to:

- compare monthly or yearly revenue
- review growth targets
- report performance
- analyze strategic changes

## Common mistakes

Common mistakes include:

- comparing different time periods unfairly
- ignoring seasonality
- using incomplete revenue data
- focusing only on growth without profit context

## Revenue growth vs profit growth

These are related but different.

- **Revenue growth** measures income growth
- **Profit growth** measures bottom-line growth

A business can grow revenue without growing profit.

## FAQs

### What is revenue growth?

Revenue growth is the percentage change in revenue between two periods.

### How do you calculate revenue growth?

Revenue Growth % = ((New Revenue − Old Revenue) ÷ Old Revenue) × 100.

### Why is revenue growth important?

It helps businesses measure momentum and compare performance over time.

### Is revenue growth always positive?

No. Revenue growth can be negative if revenue declines.
