## What this calculator does

The profit calculator helps you work out how much money is left after total costs are deducted from revenue.

It is useful for:

- business reviews
- pricing decisions
- budgeting
- product or service analysis

## Profit Formula

**Profit = Revenue − Total Costs**

Where:

- **Revenue** = total income earned
- **Total Costs** = all relevant expenses
- **Profit** = what remains after costs are covered

## Example calculation

If:

- Revenue = **5000**
- Total costs = **3200**

Then:

- Profit = **5000 − 3200**
- Profit = **1800**

## What is profit?

Profit is the amount of money left after all relevant costs are deducted from revenue.

It is one of the most important measures of business performance.

## Why profit matters

Profit matters because it helps you:

- assess business health
- make pricing decisions
- plan growth
- compare products, services, or periods

Without profit, a business cannot grow sustainably.

## When to use this calculator

Use this calculator when you need to:

- review monthly performance
- estimate earnings from a project
- compare different pricing options
- understand the impact of costs

## Common mistakes

Common mistakes include:

- forgetting hidden costs
- excluding transaction fees or tax impacts
- confusing revenue with profit
- comparing profit without considering scale

## Profit vs revenue

Profit and revenue are not the same.

- **Revenue** is total income
- **Profit** is what remains after costs

A business can have strong revenue but weak profit.
