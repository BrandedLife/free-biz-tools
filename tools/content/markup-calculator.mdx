Use this calculator to work out markup percentage based on cost and selling price.

## What this calculator does

The markup calculator helps you measure how much you are adding to cost when setting a selling price.

It is useful for:

- retail pricing
- wholesale pricing
- product costing
- supplier comparisons

## Markup Formula

**Markup (%) = ((Selling Price − Cost) ÷ Cost) × 100**

Where:

- **Cost** = what the item costs you
- **Selling Price** = what you charge the customer
- **Markup** = the percentage added to cost

## Example calculation

If:

- Cost = **500**
- Selling price = **750**

Then:

- Profit = **250**
- Markup = **(250 ÷ 500) × 100**
- Markup = **50%**

## What is markup?

Markup is the percentage added to cost to arrive at a selling price.

It tells you how much extra you are charging above cost.

## Why markup matters

Markup matters because it helps businesses:

- price products consistently
- protect profitability
- account for overheads and fees
- compare pricing strategies

## When to use this calculator

Use this calculator when you need to:

- set retail prices
- calculate wholesale pricing
- compare pricing scenarios
- check whether a supplier cost still works

## Common mistakes

Common mistakes include:

- confusing markup with margin
- forgetting fees, shipping, or tax
- using inconsistent cost figures
- pricing too low for the required overhead

## Markup vs margin

Markup and margin are not the same.

- **Markup** is calculated from cost
- **Margin** is calculated from revenue

A business should understand both before making pricing decisions.
