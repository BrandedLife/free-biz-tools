Use this calculator to estimate quick ratio.

## What this calculator does

The quick ratio calculator helps measure short-term liquidity using only the most liquid assets.

It is useful for:

- liquidity analysis
- solvency review
- lender discussions
- financial planning

## Formula

**Quick Ratio = Cash and Receivables ÷ Current Liabilities**

Where:

- **Cash and Receivables** = highly liquid short-term assets
- **Current Liabilities** = short-term obligations
- **Quick Ratio** = immediate liquidity ratio

## Example calculation

If:

- Cash and receivables = **70000**
- Current liabilities = **50000**

Then:

- Quick ratio = **70000 ÷ 50000**
- Quick ratio = **1.40**

## What is quick ratio?

Quick ratio measures how easily a business can meet short-term obligations using its most liquid assets.

## Why quick ratio matters

This calculation helps businesses:

- assess immediate liquidity
- support lender analysis
- review financial resilience
- compare periods

## When to use this calculator

Use this calculator when you want to:

- focus on liquid assets
- assess short-term solvency
- support funding discussions
- compare liquidity strength

## Common mistakes

Common mistakes include:

- including inventory in quick assets
- using inconsistent dates
- ignoring receivable quality
- comparing unlike businesses

## Quick ratio vs current ratio

These are closely related.

- **Quick ratio** focuses on highly liquid assets
- **Current ratio** includes all current assets
