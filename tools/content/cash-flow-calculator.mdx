Use this calculator to estimate net cash flow.

## What this calculator does

The cash flow calculator helps measure the difference between cash inflows and cash outflows.

It is useful for:

- cash planning
- financial forecasting
- business health review
- liquidity monitoring

## Formula

**Net Cash Flow = Cash Inflows - Cash Outflows**

Where:

- **Cash Inflows** = money coming into the business
- **Cash Outflows** = money leaving the business
- **Net Cash Flow** = cash remaining after outflows

## Example calculation

If:

- Cash inflows = **50000**
- Cash outflows = **42000**

Then:

- Net cash flow = **50000 - 42000**
- Net cash flow = **8000**

## What is net cash flow?

Net cash flow shows how much cash a business has left after all outgoing cash is deducted.

## Why net cash flow matters

This calculation helps businesses:

- monitor liquidity
- avoid cash shortages
- plan operations
- assess financial stability

## When to use this calculator

Use this calculator when you want to:

- review monthly cash position

## Common mistakes

Common mistakes include:

- confusing profit with cash
- leaving out irregular payments
- mixing time periods
- ignoring non-operating cash movement

## Cash flow vs profit

These are closely related.

- **Cash flow** measures actual money movement
- **Profit** measures income after expenses on paper
