## What this calculator does

The net margin calculator helps estimate what percentage of revenue remains as net profit.

It is useful for:

- profitability review
- financial analysis
- benchmarking
- long-term planning

## Formula

**Net Margin = Net Profit ÷ Revenue × 100**

Where:

- **Net Profit** = profit after all expenses
- **Revenue** = total income from sales
- **Net Margin** = net profit expressed as a percentage of revenue

## Example calculation

If:

- Net profit = **15000**
- Revenue = **100000**

Then:

- Net margin = **15000 ÷ 100000 × 100**
- Net margin = **15%**

## What is net margin?

Net margin shows how much profit a business keeps after all expenses are deducted from revenue.

## Why net margin matters

This calculation helps businesses:

- understand overall profitability
- compare performance over time
- benchmark against competitors
- support strategic decisions

## When to use this calculator

Use this calculator when you want to:

- review bottom-line performance
- compare periods
- assess business efficiency
- support investor or lender reporting

## Common mistakes

Common mistakes include:

- using operating profit instead of net profit
- excluding taxes or interest
- comparing mismatched periods
- focusing only on margin without looking at cash flow

## Net margin vs operating margin

These are closely related.

- **Net margin** shows final profitability after all expenses
- **Operating margin** focuses on core operating performance
