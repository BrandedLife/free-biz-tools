Use this calculator to estimate net profit based on revenue and total expenses.

## What this calculator does

The net profit calculator helps you determine how much money remains after all expenses are deducted from revenue.

It is useful for:

- business reviews
- product profitability checks
- budgeting
- financial planning

## Net Profit Formula

**Net Profit = Revenue − Total Expenses**

Where:

- **Revenue** = total income earned
- **Total Expenses** = all business costs
- **Net Profit** = what remains after expenses

## Example calculation

If:

- Revenue = **10000**
- Total expenses = **7200**

Then:

- Net profit = **10000 − 7200**
- Net profit = **2800**

## What is net profit?

Net profit is the amount left after all expenses are deducted from total revenue.

It reflects the actual profitability of a business after operating costs, fees, and other expenses are taken into account.

## Why net profit matters

Net profit helps businesses:

- measure real profitability
- evaluate business performance
- plan for growth
- compare different periods or products

## When to use this calculator

Use this calculator when you want to:

- review monthly or annual performance
- compare revenue against expenses
- estimate profits from a product or service
- check whether a business activity is worthwhile

## Common mistakes

Common mistakes include:

- confusing profit with revenue
- leaving out some expenses
- ignoring fees or taxes
- comparing net profit without context

## Net profit vs gross profit

These are related but different.

- **Gross profit** is revenue minus direct costs
- **Net profit** is what remains after all expenses

Net profit gives a more complete picture of business performance.
