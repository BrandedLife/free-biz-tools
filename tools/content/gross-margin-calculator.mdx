Use this calculator to work out gross margin based on revenue and cost of goods sold.

## What this calculator does

The gross margin calculator helps you measure how much revenue remains after direct production or product costs are deducted.

It is useful for:

- product profitability analysis
- pricing reviews
- supplier comparisons
- monthly business reporting

## Gross Margin Formula

**Gross Margin (%) = ((Revenue − Cost of Goods Sold) ÷ Revenue) × 100**

Where:

- **Revenue** = total sales income
- **Cost of Goods Sold** = direct product or production costs
- **Gross Profit** = revenue minus cost of goods sold

## Example calculation

If:

- Revenue = **1000**
- Cost of goods sold = **650**

Then:

- Gross profit = **350**
- Gross margin = **(350 ÷ 1000) × 100**
- Gross margin = **35%**

## What is gross margin?

Gross margin shows the percentage of revenue left after direct product costs are covered.

It does not include all operating expenses, only direct costs.

## Why gross margin matters

Gross margin matters because it helps you:

- understand product-level profitability
- compare supplier costs
- spot pricing problems early
- improve operational efficiency

## When to use this calculator

Use this calculator when you want to:

- review product performance
- compare categories or SKUs
- assess the impact of supplier changes
- improve pricing decisions

## Common mistakes

Common mistakes include:

- including indirect overheads in cost of goods sold
- confusing gross margin with net profit margin
- using inaccurate revenue figures
- forgetting product returns or discounts

## Gross margin vs profit margin

These two metrics are related but different.

- **Gross margin** uses direct costs only
- **Profit margin** includes a wider view of cost

Gross margin is useful for product analysis, while profit margin is better for overall profitability.
