Use this calculator to estimate refund rate.

## What this calculator does

The refund rate calculator helps measure what percentage of orders are refunded.

It is useful for:

- ecommerce reporting
- profitability review
- customer experience analysis
- operational planning

## Formula

**Refund Rate = Refunded Orders ÷ Total Orders × 100**

Where:

- **Refunded Orders** = number of refunded orders
- **Total Orders** = total completed orders
- **Refund Rate** = percentage of orders refunded

## Example calculation

If:

- Refunded orders = **12**
- Total orders = **400**

Then:

- Refund rate = **12 ÷ 400 × 100**
- Refund rate = **3%**

## What is refund rate?

Refund rate is the percentage of completed orders that end up being refunded.

## Why refund rate matters

This calculation helps businesses:

- track quality issues
- assess customer satisfaction
- protect margins
- identify product or fulfilment problems

## When to use this calculator

Use this calculator when you want to:

- review store performance
- monitor refunds
- compare products or channels
- improve profitability

## Common mistakes

Common mistakes include:

- confusing refunds with returns
- using incomplete order data
- ignoring partial refunds
- comparing different time periods

## Refund rate vs return rate

These are closely related.

- **Refund rate** measures refunded orders
- **Return rate** measures returned orders

## FAQs

### What does this calculator do?

It helps you calculate refund rate.

### Why is refund rate important?

It shows how much of your order volume is being reversed through refunds.

### Is refund rate the same as return rate?

Not always. Some returned orders are refunded, but not all refunds involve physical returns.
