## What this calculator does

The product price calculator helps you determine how much to charge for a product to achieve a specific profit target.

It considers:

- cost per unit
- markup or margin

This helps businesses set prices that support profitability.

## Product Pricing Formula

A simple pricing formula is:

**Selling Price = Cost × (1 + Markup Percentage)**

Where:

- **Cost** = total cost per unit
- **Markup** = percentage added to cost

## Example calculation

If:

- Product cost = **40**
- Desired markup = **50%**

Then:

Selling price = **40 × 1.5**

Selling price = **60**

## What is product pricing?

Product pricing is the process of determining how much customers will pay for a product.

It should reflect:

- production costs
- market demand
- competition
- desired profit margin

## Why pricing matters

Pricing affects:

- profitability
- sales volume
- market positioning
- brand perception

Even small pricing changes can have a large impact on profit.

## When to use this calculator

Use this calculator when you want to:

- price a new product
- review existing product pricing
- test different markup levels
- improve profitability

## Common mistakes

Common mistakes include:

- ignoring hidden costs
- pricing too low to cover overhead
- copying competitor prices without analysis
- confusing markup and margin

## Product price vs markup

Markup determines how much is added to cost.

Product price is the final amount customers pay.

Understanding both helps create sustainable pricing strategies.

## FAQs

### How do you calculate product price?

Selling Price = Cost × (1 + Markup).

### Why is pricing important?

Pricing affects profitability, competitiveness, and demand.

### What is a good markup?

The ideal markup depends on the industry and cost structure.

### What is the difference between markup and margin?
